Continuous Degradation Detection (CODED)

Fill: Header figure

Deforestation and forest degradation are the most pressing threat to global forests. Deforestation is the clearing of forest land for non-forest uses. In contrast, forest degradation is a reduction in the capacity of a forest to provide goods and services, characterized by a reduction in carbon storage, tree cover, biodiversity, and shifts in forest structure.

While studying for my doctorate at Boston University, I was awarded a NASA NESSF Earth and Space Science Fellowship to study remote sensing methods for monitoring forest degradation. This ultimately led to the development of the Continuous Degradation Detection (CODED) methodology, an open-source tool that uses Landsat or Sentinel-2 data to detect and map forest degradation and deforestation.

How CODED Works
1. Spectral mixture analysis to calculate the Normalized Difference Fraction Index (NDFI)
2. Harmonic regression to identify change points at the pixel level

    Forest Degradation

    I use advanced remote sensing, statistical, GIS, and machine learning techniques to map and measure forest degradation and evaluate it's social and environmental impact.

    Summary

    Forest degradation profoundly impacts the health of the world's forests and the people that depend on them. Driven primarily by human activities, forest degradation causes carbon emissions, damages ecosystems, threatens biodiversity, and reduces the ability of forests to deliver critical ecosystem services.

    Tackling this subtle yet considerable issue is complex and has been a focus of my work. My research, including developing the Continuous Degradation Detection (CODED) algorithm, offers practical tools to detect and quantify forest degradation, providing valuable insights and solutions for forest conservation, natural resource management, and supporting sustainable livelihoods.

    The following sections summarize my scientific contributions to this pressing issue, including:

    • Pioneered the Continuous Degradation Detection (CODED) Algorithm: This innovative tool detects and quantifies forest degradation, bringing the less visible but significant issue into focus.
    • Unveiled the Hidden Scale of Forest Disturbance: Produced a 30-year record of deforestation and forest degradation in the Amazon. The findings revealed that over twice the area of forests have been disturbed than previously recognized.
    • Influenced Climate Change Strategies: Nepal incorporated the CODED algorithm into their official REDD+ strategy and National Determined Contributions to the Paris Climate Agreement, demonstrating a direct application of the research in climate change mitigation efforts.
    • Collaborated with International Organizations: The World Bank adopted the CODED methodology for a study in Fiji. This collaborative work shows the practical application of the CODED algorithm in real-world scenarios.
    • Integrated into Global Platforms: The CODED algorithm was integrated into the Food and Agriculture Organization's SEPAL platform, broadening its accessibility and use.
    • Contributed to Scholarly Literature: Authored four influential research articles and contributed to two others as a co-author, spreading knowledge and insights within the academic community.
    • Educated on Forest Degradation: Conducted eight technical workshops on the use of CODED to support REDD+ projects, spreading awareness and understanding of forest degradation.
    • Developed Online Learning Resources: Created an e-Learning course, an online application, and World Bank-funded tutorials on the OpenMRV platform for the widespread use of CODED, ensuring knowledge and tools are accessible to a wider audience.

    Research Projects

    Continuous Degradation Detection (CODED)


    Background

    Satellite data monitoring of forest degradation is complex, hampered by resolution constraints, frequent cloud cover, and the challenge of differentiating natural variability from actual degradation. Scroll through the examples below to see for yourself.

    Further complications arise from potential errors in change detection algorithms and the inherently diverse nature of degradation processes, necessitating flexible and sophisticated monitoring strategies.

    Description

    I designed CODED to address these challenges by coupling time-series analysis of satellite imagery with supervised machine learning, allowing it to track degradation events through time.


    Resources

    The CODED algorithm works by:

    • 1. Applying a spectral mixture model to all available Landsat data
    • • Generating a 30-year record of deforestation and forest degradation in the Amazon, showing that the over twice the area of forests have been disturbed than previously recognized.
    • • Use of my CODED algorithm by Nepal to set an emissions baseline for their official REDD+ strategy and National Determined Contributions to the Paris Climate Agreement
